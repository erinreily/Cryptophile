Mary Queen of Scots

We'll take a break from the encryption and decryption for a short history lesson. Beginning with her inheritance of the Scottish throne at six days old, Mary Queen of Scots had a tumultuous life, with multiple marriages, several unsuccessful attempts to secure her rule over Scotland, all ending with her last eighteen and a half years imprisoned by Queen Elizabeth, her cousin. During her captivity, she remained active and sent and received multiple encrypted letters to contribute to the Babington Plot, a plan to assassinate Elizabeth. Unfortunately for Mary, her cipher wasn't strong enough-- her letters were intercepted and decrypted, which eventually led to her beheading. Had her cipher been more effective, she would have had grounds to defend herself. A good cipher is really important!
