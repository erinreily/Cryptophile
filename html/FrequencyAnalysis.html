Frequency Analysis

One way that monoalphabetic substitution was combated was with a technique called frequency analysis. The cryptanalyst uses the average frequency with which each letter appears in the English alphabet to get started on decrypting a message. For example, the letter e is the most common in the alphabet, appearing 12.7% of the time! E is followed by t at 9.1%, a at 8.2%, and o at 7.5%, with z trailing at 0.07%. Logic follows that the most frequent letter in the encrypted message, be it q, y, or anything else, will represent e. It's also a safe bet that the 3 next most frequent letters in the encrypted message will represent t, a, and o.


Frequency analysis works best with longer passages so that the average frequencies are more likely to line up with unencrypted letter frequencies. Try deciphering the passage below: one letter will have a frequency of around 15%, and it's a safe bet that that's our e!
