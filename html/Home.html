Introduction

Ever bought something online? Logged into a website? Withdrew money from an ATM? You've relied on cryptography to keep your information safe. But how does it work and where did it come from? Cryptography has a long and rich history extending from at least 600 BCE to the present. From simple monoalphabetic substitutions to quantum cryptography, it's saved lives and doomed others, started and ended wars, and been an area of intense study for the cryptanalysts that work to break ciphers.


This website was created to provide an overview of cryptography throughout the ages in a concise, engaging way. Use the tabs above to navigate to important milestones in crypto-history. Following each short lesson are puzzles or mini-games to exercise your new understanding of cryptographic techniques.


Inspiration for this site is duly credited to "The Code Book" by Simon Singh.
